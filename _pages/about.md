### About me

**Hi there, I am Yanchu Zhang :mortar_board:**

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering at Northeastern University, working with [Dr. Ruobing Bai](https://coe.northeastern.edu/people/bai-ruobing/). My research centers on computational mechanics, with broader interests in multiphysics coupling, data-driven methods, high-performance computing (HPC), and scientific open-source software development.

Prior to joining Northeastern, I received my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering in 2025 from the [University of Southern California](https://www.usc.edu/), where I worked with [Dr. Qiming Wang](https://viterbi.usc.edu/directory/faculty/Wang/Qiming) on bio-inspired mechanics and constitutive modeling of soft materials and polymer networks. I previously earned an M.S. in Engineering Mechanics in 2020 from [Columbia University](https://www.columbia.edu/) under the supervision of [Dr. Huiming Yin](https://www.engineering.columbia.edu/faculty-staff/directory/huiming-yin). Together, these experiences established my academic foundation in solid mechanics, mathematical modeling, and computational methods for complex material behavior.

I also received an M.S. in Applied Mathematics in 2025 from the University of Southern California, where I developed a stronger foundation in measure-theoretical probability, statistical inference, large-sample theory, and stochastic modeling. This training expanded my research interests beyond mechanics and further shaped my approach to quantitative trading, derivative pricing, and data-driven methods for quantitative finance.

### Research interest

- **Finite element analysis** of coupled thermo-mechanical and multiphysics systems, with interests in **fracture mechanics**, **phase-field approaches**, **heat transfer**, **multiphase flow**, and **UMAT/VUMAT**-based constitutive model implementation.

- **Parameter identification** and **uncertainty quantification** for inverse problems in multiphysics systems through **deep learning** approaches and the **adjoint variable method**.
- **Constitutive modeling** of **polymer networks**, **hyperelastic biological tissues**, **advanced IC packaging materials**, and **phase-change materials** for predictive simulation of complex material behavior.
- **Machine learning**, **statistical learning**, and **time series modeling** for **quantitative finance**, with an emphasis on forecasting, alpha generation, signal extraction, and data-driven modeling of financial markets.
- **Scientific software development** and **object-oriented programming (OOP)** for computational mechanics, finite element simulation, and high-performance scientific computing.
